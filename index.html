Addition

As the name tells us, addition means that you just have to add any item. It is very simple. You will understand better when we will check out the examples. Now lets check some important points regarding Addition.

1. Whenever we add 'zero' to some number, we always get the same number.
2. We can always compare the addition problems with our real life problems.

Now let us try some examples.

Apples

If you have 3 apples with you and your mother gives you another 3 apples, how many apples will you have together?

The total number of apples will be 3+3=6.
Now you have 6 apples!!!.

Oranges

If you have 4 oranges and your friend gives you 1 orange, how many oranges will you have finally?

The total number of oranges will be 4+1=5.
Now you have 5 yummy oranges!!!.

Pencils

If you have 7 pencils in your box and your brother gives you another 8 pencils, how many pencils will you have?

The total number of pencils will be 7+8=15.
Now you have 15 pencils!!!.

Balls

If you are playing with 2 balls and your father gives you another 5 balls, how many balls do you have now?>

The total number of balls you will have is 2+5=7.
So now you have 7 balls to play!!!.

Subtraction

Subtraction means that you have to remove some items from some given items. This simply means that you have to take away some number from another number. Let us see some points about subtraction.

1. Whenever we subtract 'zero' from a number, we get the same number.
2. When we subtract the number from itself, we will get the answer as 'zero'.

Now let us check some examples.

Erasers

Suppose you have 9 erasers with you. Your sister asked for 3 erasers from you. How many erasers will you have after giving the erasers to your sister?

When we remove 3 erasers from 9 erasers, we are left with 9-3=6 erasers.

Chocolates

If you have 20 chocolates and you give 10 chocolates to your brother, how many chocolates do you have now?

When your brother takes 10 chocolates from your 20 chocolates you will have only 20-10=10 chocolates:(

Biscuits

If your friend has 10 biscuits and you take 4 biscuits from him, how many biscuits will he have?

When you take 4 biscuits from his 10 biscuits he will only have 10-4=6 biscuits for him.

Books

If you have 5 books and you give all the five books to your friend, how many books do you have now?

When you give your 5 books to your friend, you will have 5-5=0 books with you.

Multiplication

Multiplication means repeated additon. When you add something to another thing continuously, it is called multiplication. Lets us look at an example so that you will understand better.

Example: If one bag has 5 apples, then how many apples are there in 6 bags?
We can see that one bag has 5 apples. So 2 bags will have 5+5=10 apples, 3 bags will have 5+5+5=15 apples, 4 bags will have 5+5+5+5=20 apples, 5 bags will have 5+5+5+5+5=25 apples, and finally 6 bags will have 5+5+5+5+5+5=30 apples.
Here we have added 5 'six' times. So 5 multiplied by 6 will be 5 x 6 = 30.

Let us look at some other examples also.

Pens

If 1 packet of pens has 10 pens, then how many pens are there in 6 packets?

The number of pens in 6 packets are 10+10+10+10+10+10=60 pens, which is also same as 6x10=60 pens.

Sharpeners

If 1 box of sharpeners has 5 sharpeners in it, then how many sharpeners will be there in 8 boxes?

The number of sharpeners in 8 boxes are 5+5+5+5+5+5+5+5=40 sharpeners, which is same as 5x8=40 sharpeners.

Bananas

If 1 basket of bananas has 7 bananas, then how many bananas are there in 7 baskets?

The number of bananas in 7 baskets are 7+7+7+7+7+7+7=49 bananas which is equal to 7x7=49 bananas.

Rasgullas

If one box of rasagullas has 8 rasagullas, then how many rasagullas will be there in 10 boxes?

The number of rasagullas in 10 boxes are 8+8+8+8+8+8+8+8+8+8=80 rasagullas which is same as 8x10=80 rasagullas.

Division

Division means dividing something into some equal parts. When you divide a number with anothe number it means that you are cutting the the thing into some number of equal parts. You will understand this when you will see some examples.

Pizza

If a pizza is cut into 6 slices and there are 3 people in the room, then we say that each person gets 2 slices of pizza. This is called division. We can also say that 6 divided by 3 is 2.

Cake

If a cake is cut into 8 pieces and there are only 2 people in the room, then we say that each person will get 4 pieces of cake. We can also say this as 8 divided by 2 is 4.

Pie

If we cut a pie into 10 equal parts and there are 5 people in the party, then if we divide the pie among 5 people, every person will get 2 pieces of pie. This can be said as 10 divided by 5 is 2.

Strawberries

If you have 12 strawberries and there are 4 people in the room then, if we divide all the strawberries equally then every person will get 3 strawberries. This is also said as 12 divide by 4 is 3.

We have learnt addition, subtraction, multiplication, and division with the help of so many real life examples.
